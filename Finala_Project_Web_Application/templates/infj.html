Brief Introduction

INFJs live in the world of ideas. They were independent, original thinkers with strong feelings, strong principles, and upright humanity. INFJs trust their own opinions and decisions, even in the face of doubt. Some books refer to INFJs as "consultants", "writers" and "guardians," but I think it's better to call them "guides".

INFJ's key traits are deep, mysterious, stubborn, loyal, and driven. They may be the rarest personality type of all, but they certainly leave their mark on the world. Idealistic and principled, they aren’t content to coast through life – they want to stand up and make a difference.
